Scoring trust — where we actually stand

Held-out vs owner
1.29

MAE on 70 graded hotels, scored with the answer hidden. 56% within 1pt, 81% within 2pt. Near the floor where humans disagree with each other (~1–1.5). The scorer genuinely learned your taste.

Friends agree with friends
84%

Inter-rater agreement across 5 raters, 121 votes (felicia, theo, pelle, malin, rikard). Cosiness is a real, shared signal — not random.

AI vs friend consensus
0.56

Correlation between AI score and the friends’ cosy-vote. Moderate — the AI matches you well but a broader panel only so-so. The fix: feed friend consensus into calibration, not just owner grades.

How scoring works: one model (Claude) weighs the words (name, amenities, reviews) + the photo + your graded taste into a single 0–10 → normalised to honest pins (typical 5.0 / good 6.5 / exceptional 8.5). A free local model (Qwen-7B, warmth MAE 1.0) can do the photo read for $0; a heuristic gate rejects junk images first.
